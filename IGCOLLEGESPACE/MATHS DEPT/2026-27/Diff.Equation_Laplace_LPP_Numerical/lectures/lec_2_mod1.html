🎯 1. Learning Outcomes (CO1)
  • Recognize and solve separable ordinary differential equations
  • Formulate mathematical models using ODEs (population growth, decay, cooling, mixing)
  • Apply initial conditions to find particular solutions
📝 2. Separable ODEs – Definition & Method
A first-order ODE is called separable if it can be written in the form: \[ \frac{dy}{dx} = g(x) \cdot h(y) \quad \text{or} \quad M(x)\,dx + N(y)\,dy = 0. \] Solution method: Separate variables and integrate: \[ \int \frac{dy}{h(y)} = \int g(x)\,dx + C. \]
Example (Kreyszig 1.3): Solve \(\frac{dy}{dx} = x y\).
Separate: \[ \frac{dy}{y} = x\,dx \] Integrate: \[ \ln|y| = \frac{x^2}{2} + C \quad \Rightarrow \quad y = A e^{x^2/2}. \]
🧪 3. Modeling with Differential Equations

Many real-world processes lead to separable ODEs. Below are the fundamental models with detailed equations:

📈 Exponential Growth/Decay Model
The rate of change of a quantity \(y\) is proportional to the amount present: \[ \frac{dy}{dt} = k y \] where \(k > 0\) for growth and \(k < 0\) for decay.
Solution: \[ y(t) = y_0 e^{kt} \] Applications: Population growth (unlimited resources), radioactive decay, compound interest, drug absorption.
🌡️ Newton's Law of Cooling/Warming
The rate of change of temperature \(T\) of an object is proportional to the difference between its temperature and the ambient temperature \(T_a\): \[ \frac{dT}{dt} = k(T - T_a) \] where \(k\) is a constant (negative for cooling).
Solution: \[ T(t) = T_a + (T_0 - T_a)e^{kt} \] Example: A hot coffee cooling in a room, forensic science to determine time of death.
💧 Mixing Problems (Tank Problems)
For a tank with brine solution, let \(A(t)\) be the amount of salt. The rate equation: \[ \frac{dA}{dt} = \text{Rate in} - \text{Rate out} \] If inflow concentration \(c_i\) and flow rate \(r_i\), outflow rate \(r_o\) and volume \(V(t)\): \[ \frac{dA}{dt} = c_i r_i - \frac{A}{V} r_o \] This often reduces to a linear or separable ODE.
🦠 Logistic Growth Model (Limited Resources)
Population growth with carrying capacity \(K\): \[ \frac{dP}{dt} = r P \left(1 - \frac{P}{K}\right) \] This is separable and yields the logistic curve: \[ P(t) = \frac{K}{1 + \left(\frac{K}{P_0} - 1\right)e^{-rt}} \] Applications: Epidemiology, fishery management, bacterial growth in a petri dish.
⚛️ Radioactive Decay & Half-Life
For a radioactive substance, \[ \frac{dN}{dt} = -\lambda N \] The half-life \(T_{1/2}\) satisfies: \[ T_{1/2} = \frac{\ln 2}{\lambda} \] Solution: \[ N(t) = N_0 e^{-\lambda t} \] Used in carbon dating and nuclear medicine.
🔍 4. Procedure for Modeling
StepDescription
1Identify the dependent and independent variables
2Express the rate of change as a derivative
3Set up equation based on physical law (growth, cooling, etc.)
4Solve the ODE (often separable)
5Apply initial condition to find particular solution
📚 Workout Examples (From Question Bank)

Problems on separable ODEs, modeling, and solution verification. Click Answer to see step-by-step solution.

1. Solve the differential equation \(\frac{dy}{dx} = (x + 1)e^{-x}y^2\). CO1
✅ Step-by-step solution: This is separable. Rewrite: \[ \frac{dy}{y^2} = (x+1)e^{-x} dx \] Integrate: \[ \int y^{-2} dy = \int (x+1)e^{-x} dx \] Left side: \(-y^{-1} = -1/y\). Right side integrate by parts: \(\int (x+1)e^{-x} dx = -(x+2)e^{-x} + C\).
Thus: \[ -\frac{1}{y} = -(x+2)e^{-x} + C \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{1}{y} = (x+2)e^{-x} + C \] Hence: \[ y = \frac{1}{(x+2)e^{-x} + C} \]
2. Solve the initial value problem \(y' = -2xy\), \(y(0) = 1.8\). CO1
✅ Solution: Separable: \[ \frac{dy}{y} = -2x\,dx \] Integrate: \[ \ln|y| = -x^2 + C \quad \Rightarrow \quad y = A e^{-x^2} \] Using \(y(0)=1.8\): \[ 1.8 = A e^{0} = A \quad \Rightarrow \quad A = 1.8 \] Particular solution: \[ y = 1.8 e^{-x^2} \]
3. Solve \(y' = Ay - By^2\) (Logistic model). Interpret the solution. CO1
✅ Solution: This is a Bernoulli equation but separable. Write: \[ \frac{dy}{dt} = y(A - By) \] Separate: \[ \frac{dy}{y(A-By)} = dt \] Partial fractions: \[ \frac{1}{y(A-By)} = \frac{1}{A}\left(\frac{1}{y} + \frac{B}{A-By}\right) \] Integrate: \[ \frac{1}{A}(\ln|y| - \ln|A-By|) = t + C \] Thus: \[ \ln\left|\frac{y}{A-By}\right| = A t + C' \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{y}{A-By} = Ce^{At} \] Solve for \(y\): \[ y = \frac{A}{B + k e^{-At}} \] Carrying capacity = \(A/B\).
4. Solve \(xy' = x + y\). CO1
✅ Solution: Rewrite: \[ y' - \frac{1}{x}y = 1 \] Integrating factor: \[ \mu = e^{-\int \frac{1}{x}dx} = e^{-\ln x} = \frac{1}{x} \] Multiply: \[ \frac{1}{x}y' - \frac{1}{x^2}y = \frac{1}{x} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{y}{x}\right) = \frac{1}{x} \] Integrate: \[ \frac{y}{x} = \ln|x| + C \quad \Rightarrow \quad y = x\ln|x| + Cx \]
5. Radioactive decay: Half-life is 100 years. Find time when 20% of original remains. CO1
✅ Modeling solution: \[ \frac{dA}{dt} = -kA \quad \Rightarrow \quad A(t) = A_0 e^{-kt} \] Half-life \(T_{1/2}=100\): \[ 0.5 = e^{-k\cdot 100} \quad \Rightarrow \quad k = \frac{\ln 2}{100} \] For 20% remaining: \[ 0.2 = e^{-kt} \quad \Rightarrow \quad t = \frac{\ln 5}{k} = 100\frac{\ln 5}{\ln 2} \approx 232.5 \text{ years} \]
6. Newton's Law of Cooling: Body at 100°C in room at 25°C, after 10 min at 80°C. Find time to reach 50°C. CO1
✅ Solution: Newton's Law: \[ \frac{dT}{dt} = k(T-25) \] Separate: \[ \frac{dT}{T-25} = k\,dt \] Integrate: \[ \ln|T-25| = kt + C \] \(T(0)=100\) gives \(C = \ln 75\). So: \[ T-25 = 75 e^{kt} \] At \(t=10\), \(T=80\): \[ 55 = 75 e^{10k} \quad \Rightarrow \quad e^{10k} = 0.7333 \quad \Rightarrow \quad k \approx -0.031 \] For \(T=50\): \[ 25 = 75 e^{kt} \quad \Rightarrow \quad e^{kt} = \frac{1}{3} \quad \Rightarrow \quad t = \frac{\ln(1/3)}{k} \approx 35.4 \text{ minutes} \]
7. Solve the mixing problem: Tank with 100 L pure water. Brine (0.2 kg/L) enters at 5 L/min, mixture drains at 5 L/min. Find salt after t minutes. CO1
✅ Modeling: Let \(S(t)\) = salt (kg). Rate in = \(0.2 \times 5 = 1\) kg/min. Rate out = \(\frac{S}{100} \times 5 = S/20\). \[ \frac{dS}{dt} = 1 - \frac{S}{20} \] Separate: \[ \frac{dS}{1 - S/20} = dt \] Integrate: \[ -20\ln\left|1 - \frac{S}{20}\right| = t + C \] \(S(0)=0\) → \(C = 0\). Then: \[ 1 - \frac{S}{20} = e^{-t/20} \quad \Rightarrow \quad S(t) = 20\left(1 - e^{-t/20}\right) \text{ kg} \]
8. Solve \(2xy y' = y^2 - x^2\) by reducing to separable form. CO1
✅ Solution: This is homogeneous. Let \(y = vx\) → \(y' = v + xv'\). Substitute: \[ 2x(vx)(v + xv') = (vx)^2 - x^2 \] Simplify: \[ 2vx^2(v + xv') = v^2x^2 - x^2 \quad \Rightarrow \quad 2v(v + xv') = v^2 - 1 \] \[ 2v^2 + 2v xv' = v^2 - 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad 2v xv' = -v^2 - 1 \] Separate: \[ \frac{2v}{1+v^2} dv = -\frac{dx}{x} \] Integrate: \[ \ln(1+v^2) = -\ln|x| + C \quad \Rightarrow \quad 1+v^2 = \frac{C}{x} \] Substitute back \(v = y/x\): \[ 1 + \frac{y^2}{x^2} = \frac{C}{x} \quad \Rightarrow \quad x^2 + y^2 = Cx \]
