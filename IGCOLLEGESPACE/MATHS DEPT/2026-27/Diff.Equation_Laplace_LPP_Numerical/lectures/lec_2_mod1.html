🧪 2. What is Mathematical Modeling?
Mathematical Modeling is the process of translating real-world phenomena into mathematical equations. The steps are:
  1. Identify the relevant quantities and variables
  2. Establish relationships (laws, principles) governing the system
  3. Formulate a differential equation
  4. Solve the ODE
  5. Interpret the solution back to the real world
📈 3. Exponential Growth/Decay Model
The simplest and most fundamental model states that the rate of change of a quantity \(y\) is proportional to the amount present: \[ \frac{dy}{dt} = k y \] where:
  • \(k > 0\) → exponential growth
  • \(k < 0\) → exponential decay
The general solution is: \[ y(t) = y_0 e^{kt} \] where \(y_0 = y(0)\) is the initial amount.
🔬 Application 1: Population Growth (Malthusian Model)
If a population grows at a rate proportional to its size, then: \[ \frac{dP}{dt} = r P, \quad P(0) = P_0 \] Solution: \[ P(t) = P_0 e^{rt} \] where \(r\) is the growth rate (birth rate − death rate).
⚛️ Application 2: Radioactive Decay
Radioactive substances decay at a rate proportional to the amount present: \[ \frac{dN}{dt} = -\lambda N, \quad N(0) = N_0 \] Solution: \[ N(t) = N_0 e^{-\lambda t} \] where \(\lambda > 0\) is the decay constant. The half-life \(T_{1/2}\) is the time for half of the substance to decay: \[ T_{1/2} = \frac{\ln 2}{\lambda} \]
🔍 4. Modeling Procedure Summary
StepAction
1Identify the dependent variable (quantity that changes) and independent variable (usually time \(t\))
2Express the rate of change as a derivative \(\frac{dy}{dt}\)
3Set up equation: rate of change = (rate in) − (rate out) or proportional relationship
4Solve the ODE (often separable or linear)
5Apply initial condition to find particular solution
📚 Workout Examples

Problems on modeling with exponential growth and decay. Click Answer to see step-by-step solution.

1. Solve the initial value problem \(y' = -2xy\), \(y(0) = 1.8\). CO1
✅ Step-by-step solution:

Step 1: Separate variables: \[ \frac{dy}{y} = -2x \, dx \] Step 2: Integrate both sides: \[ \int \frac{dy}{y} = \int -2x \, dx \] Step 3: Perform integration: \[ \ln|y| = -x^2 + C \] Step 4: Solve for \(y\): \[ y = A e^{-x^2}, \quad \text{where } A = \pm e^C \] Step 5: Apply initial condition \(y(0) = 1.8\): \[ 1.8 = A e^{0} = A \quad \Rightarrow \quad A = 1.8 \] Final answer: \[ y = 1.8 e^{-x^2} \]
2. Solve the initial value problem \(xy' + y = 0\), \(y(4) = 6\). CO1
✅ Step-by-step solution:

Step 1: Rewrite as: \[ \frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{y}{x} \] Step 2: Separate variables: \[ \frac{dy}{y} = -\frac{dx}{x} \] Step 3: Integrate: \[ \ln|y| = -\ln|x| + C \] Step 4: Simplify: \[ y = \frac{C}{x} \] Step 5: Apply initial condition \(y(4) = 6\): \[ 6 = \frac{C}{4} \quad \Rightarrow \quad C = 24 \] Final answer: \[ y = \frac{24}{x} \]
3. Solve the logistic growth model \(y' = Ay - By^2\). CO1
✅ Step-by-step solution:

Step 1: Rewrite as: \[ \frac{dy}{dt} = y(A - By) \] Step 2: Separate variables: \[ \frac{dy}{y(A-By)} = dt \] Step 3: Using partial fractions: \[ \frac{1}{y(A-By)} = \frac{1}{A}\left(\frac{1}{y} + \frac{B}{A-By}\right) \] Step 4: Integrate: \[ \frac{1}{A}(\ln|y| - \ln|A-By|) = t + C \] Step 5: Simplify: \[ \ln\left|\frac{y}{A-By}\right| = A t + C' \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{y}{A-By} = Ce^{At} \] Step 6: Solve for \(y\): \[ y = \frac{A}{B + k e^{-At}} \] where \(k = \frac{A - By_0}{y_0}\). This is the logistic growth curve with carrying capacity \(A/B\).
⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
MistakeCorrection
Confusing growth and decay (sign of \(k\))\(k > 0\) for growth, \(k < 0\) for decay
Forgetting to use initial conditionAlways apply \(y(0) = y_0\) to find particular solution
Misinterpreting half-life formula\(T_{1/2} = \frac{\ln 2}{\lambda}\), not \(\frac{0.5}{\lambda}\)
📋 Quick Summary
  • Exponential Growth/Decay Model: \[ \frac{dy}{dt} = ky \quad \Rightarrow \quad y(t) = y_0 e^{kt} \]
  • Population Growth: \[ P(t) = P_0 e^{rt} \]
  • Radioactive Decay: \[ N(t) = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}, \quad T_{1/2} = \frac{\ln 2}{\lambda} \]
  • Modeling Steps: Identify variables → Set up ODE → Solve → Interpret
