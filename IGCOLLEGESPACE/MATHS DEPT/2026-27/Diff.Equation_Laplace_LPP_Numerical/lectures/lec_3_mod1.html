📌 Module I | First Order Ordinary Differential Equations
📖 Topic: Separable ODEs – Method, Standard Form & Examples
📚 Textbook: Advanced Engineering Mathematics – E. Kreyszig | Section 1.3
👨‍🏫 Faculty: Gafoor I 🎯 CO1: Solve separable differential equations ⏱️ Lecture duration: 1 Hour
🎯 1. Learning Outcomes (CO1)
  • Recognize a separable differential equation
  • Apply the separation of variables method
  • Solve separable ODEs step by step
  • Find particular solutions using initial conditions
📌 2. Definition of a Separable ODE
A first-order ODE is called separable if it can be written in the form: \[ \frac{dy}{dx} = g(x) \cdot h(y) \] or equivalently: \[ M(x)\,dx + N(y)\,dy = 0 \] where \(M\) is a function of \(x\) only and \(N\) is a function of \(y\) only.
📝 3. Solution Method – Step by Step
Procedure to solve a separable ODE:
  1. Write the ODE in the form \(\frac{dy}{dx} = g(x) \cdot h(y)\).
  2. Separate the variables: bring all \(y\) terms to one side and all \(x\) terms to the other side. \[ \frac{1}{h(y)}\,dy = g(x)\,dx \]
  3. Integrate both sides with respect to their variables: \[ \int \frac{1}{h(y)}\,dy = \int g(x)\,dx + C \]
  4. Solve for \(y\) explicitly if possible.
  5. If an initial condition is given, substitute to find the particular solution.
📖 4. Worked Examples
Example 1 (Kreyszig 1.3): Solve \(\frac{dy}{dx} = x y\).

Step 1: Separate variables: \[ \frac{dy}{y} = x\,dx \] Step 2: Integrate both sides: \[ \int \frac{dy}{y} = \int x\,dx \] Step 3: Perform integration: \[ \ln|y| = \frac{x^2}{2} + C \] Step 4: Solve for \(y\): \[ y = A e^{x^2/2}, \quad \text{where } A = \pm e^C \]
Example 2: Solve \(\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x}{y}\) with \(y(0) = 2\).

Step 1: Separate variables: \[ y\,dy = x\,dx \] Step 2: Integrate: \[ \int y\,dy = \int x\,dx \] Step 3: Perform integration: \[ \frac{y^2}{2} = \frac{x^2}{2} + C \quad \Rightarrow \quad y^2 = x^2 + 2C \] Step 4: Apply initial condition \(y(0)=2\): \[ 4 = 0 + 2C \quad \Rightarrow \quad 2C = 4 \quad \Rightarrow \quad C = 2 \] Step 5: Particular solution: \[ y^2 = x^2 + 4 \quad \Rightarrow \quad y = \sqrt{x^2 + 4} \]
📚 Workout Examples

Problems on separable differential equations. Click Answer to see step-by-step solution.

1. Solve the ODE \(\frac{dy}{dx} = \cos x\). CO1
✅ Step-by-step solution:

Step 1: Separate variables: \[ dy = \cos x \, dx \] Step 2: Integrate both sides: \[ \int dy = \int \cos x \, dx \] Step 3: Perform integration: \[ y = \sin x + C \] Final answer: \(y = \sin x + C\)
2. Solve the ODE \(\frac{dy}{dx} = 1 + y^2\). CO1
✅ Step-by-step solution:

Step 1: Separate variables: \[ \frac{dy}{1 + y^2} = dx \] Step 2: Integrate both sides: \[ \int \frac{dy}{1 + y^2} = \int dx \] Step 3: Perform integration: \[ \tan^{-1}(y) = x + C \] Step 4: Solve for \(y\): \[ y = \tan(x + C) \]
3. Solve the differential equation \(\frac{dy}{dx} = (x + 1)e^{-x}y^2\). CO1
✅ Step-by-step solution:

Step 1: Separate variables: \[ \frac{dy}{y^2} = (x+1)e^{-x} dx \] Step 2: Integrate both sides: \[ \int y^{-2} dy = \int (x+1)e^{-x} dx \] Step 3: Left side: \[ \int y^{-2} dy = -y^{-1} = -\frac{1}{y} \] Step 4: Right side (integration by parts): Let \(u = x+1\), \(dv = e^{-x}dx\). \[ \int (x+1)e^{-x} dx = -(x+1)e^{-x} - \int -e^{-x} dx = -(x+1)e^{-x} - e^{-x} + C = -(x+2)e^{-x} + C \] Step 5: Equate: \[ -\frac{1}{y} = -(x+2)e^{-x} + C \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{1}{y} = (x+2)e^{-x} + C \] Step 6: Solve for \(y\): \[ y = \frac{1}{(x+2)e^{-x} + C} \]
4. Solve the initial value problem \(y' = -2xy\), \(y(0) = 1.8\). CO1
✅ Step-by-step solution:

Step 1: Separate variables: \[ \frac{dy}{y} = -2x \, dx \] Step 2: Integrate both sides: \[ \int \frac{dy}{y} = \int -2x \, dx \] Step 3: Perform integration: \[ \ln|y| = -x^2 + C \] Step 4: Solve for \(y\): \[ y = A e^{-x^2}, \quad \text{where } A = \pm e^C \] Step 5: Apply initial condition \(y(0) = 1.8\): \[ 1.8 = A e^{0} = A \quad \Rightarrow \quad A = 1.8 \] Final answer: \[ y = 1.8 e^{-x^2} \]
5. Solve \(2xy y' = y^2 - x^2\) by reducing it to separable form. CO1
✅ Step-by-step solution (Homogeneous equation):

Step 1: Rewrite: \[ y' = \frac{y^2 - x^2}{2xy} \] Step 2: This is homogeneous. Let \(y = vx\) ⇒ \(y' = v + xv'\).

Step 3: Substitute: \[ v + xv' = \frac{v^2 x^2 - x^2}{2x(vx)} = \frac{v^2 - 1}{2v} \] Step 4: Simplify: \[ xv' = \frac{v^2 - 1}{2v} - v = \frac{v^2 - 1 - 2v^2}{2v} = \frac{-1 - v^2}{2v} \] Step 5: Separate variables: \[ \frac{2v}{1+v^2} dv = -\frac{dx}{x} \] Step 6: Integrate: \[ \ln(1+v^2) = -\ln|x| + C \] Step 7: Simplify: \[ 1+v^2 = \frac{C}{x} \quad \Rightarrow \quad 1 + \frac{y^2}{x^2} = \frac{C}{x} \] Step 8: Multiply by \(x^2\): \[ x^2 + y^2 = Cx \]
6. Solve \(xy' = x + y\). CO1
✅ Step-by-step solution:

Step 1: Rewrite: \[ y' = 1 + \frac{y}{x} \] Let \(y = vx\) ⇒ \(y' = v + xv'\).

Step 2: Substitute: \[ v + xv' = 1 + v \quad \Rightarrow \quad xv' = 1 \] Step 3: Separate variables: \[ dv = \frac{dx}{x} \] Step 4: Integrate: \[ v = \ln|x| + C \] Step 5: Substitute back \(v = y/x\): \[ \frac{y}{x} = \ln|x| + C \quad \Rightarrow \quad y = x \ln|x| + Cx \]
⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
MistakeCorrection
Forgetting to separate variables before integrating Always isolate \(dy\) and \(dx\) on opposite sides
Omitting the constant of integration \(C\) Always add \(+ C\) after integration
Losing solutions when dividing by a function that could be zero Check for singular solutions separately
📋 Quick Summary
  • Separable ODE: \(\frac{dy}{dx} = g(x)h(y)\) or \(M(x)dx + N(y)dy = 0\)
  • Solution method: \[ \int \frac{dy}{h(y)} = \int g(x)\,dx + C \]
  • Always check for: Singular solutions, initial conditions
