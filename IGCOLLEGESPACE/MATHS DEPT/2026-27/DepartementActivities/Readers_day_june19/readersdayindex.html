🇮🇳 National Reading Day – History & Immense Significance

June 19 marks the remembrance of Puthuvayil Narayana Panicker, the father of the library movement in Kerala. In 1996, India declared this day as National Reading Day to honor his visionary efforts that ignited a mass literacy and library culture across the nation. The entire week following June 19 is observed as Reading Week, and the month as Reading Month, mobilizing millions to rediscover the joy of books.

Why does it matter? In an age of digital noise, deep reading cultivates critical thinking, empathy, and imagination. Reading is not just an academic skill — it is the doorway to lifelong learning, informed citizenship, and mental well-being. National Reading Day reminds us that every turned page builds a more thoughtful, progressive society. For students, reading habits enhance analytical reasoning, vocabulary, and concentration — core pillars for mathematics and scientific temper. 📖⚡

The Department of Mathematics, NAM College, embraces this spirit — merging rigorous logic with the boundless universe of stories. “Reading Beyond Limits” pushes us to explore narratives that fuel curiosity, just like the infinite cosmos of numbers and patterns.

🐧💡 KNOWLEDGE WANTS TO BE FREE: THE FREE SOFTWARE MOVEMENT & SAYAHNA FOUNDATION
Democratizing access to literature — the spirit of digital emancipation

The Free Software Movement, spearheaded by Richard Stallman in 1983, champions the fundamental rights of users: to run, study, share, and modify software without restrictions. It’s not merely about “free of cost” but about freedom and ethics — ensuring technology serves humanity, not corporate silos. This philosophy fuels transparency, collaboration, and equitable access to knowledge tools, which is the cornerstone of digital independence.

Sayahna Foundation (sayahna.org) embodies this radical openness in the literary and cultural sphere. Inspired by free software ideals, Sayahna offers thousands of Malayalam and regional language books, stories, and scholarly works under free licenses (CC-BY-SA or similar). No paywalls, no DRM — pure, unrestricted access to knowledge. Their mission aligns perfectly with P.N. Panicker’s vision: books should reach every home, every reader, without barriers.

📡 sayahna.org as part of this movement: It represents a living library where classic and contemporary Malayalam literature is preserved and shared freely. By accessing the story from Sayahna, we honour the free culture movement — reading without proprietary restrictions, respecting open knowledge. The platform encourages re-use, sharing, and creative adaptation, building a collective intellectual commons. As part of our Infinity Initiative, we invite students to embrace platforms like Sayahna, fostering awareness about free software and ethical digital citizenship.
