Solution 1: \(\displaystyle \int_0^{\pi/6}\sin^6 3x \, dx\)
Step 1: Substitute \(t = 3x\). Then \(dt = 3dx \Rightarrow dx = \frac{dt}{3}\).
Step 2: Change limits: when \(x = 0\), \(t = 0\); when \(x = \frac{\pi}{6}\), \(t = \frac{\pi}{2}\).
Step 3: Integral becomes \(\frac{1}{3}\int_0^{\pi/2} \sin^6 t \, dt\).
Step 4: Use reduction formula: \(\int_0^{\pi/2} \sin^n t \, dt = \frac{n-1}{n} \cdot \frac{n-3}{n-2} \cdots \times \begin{cases} \frac{\pi}{2} & n\text{ even} \\ 1 & n\text{ odd} \end{cases}\)
Step 5: For \(n=6\) (even): \(\frac{5}{6} \cdot \frac{3}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{\pi}{2} = \frac{5\pi}{32}\)
Step 6: Multiply by \(\frac{1}{3}\): \(\frac{1}{3} \times \frac{5\pi}{32} = \frac{5\pi}{96}\)
\(\boxed{\displaystyle \frac{5\pi}{96}}\)

Solution 2: \(\displaystyle \int_0^{\pi}\sin^5\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)dx\)
Step 1: Substitute \(t = \frac{x}{2}\). Then \(dt = \frac{dx}{2} \Rightarrow dx = 2dt\).
Step 2: Change limits: when \(x = 0\), \(t = 0\); when \(x = \pi\), \(t = \frac{\pi}{2}\).
Step 3: Integral becomes \(2\int_0^{\pi/2} \sin^5 t \, dt\).
Step 4: For \(n=5\) (odd): \(\frac{4}{5} \cdot \frac{2}{3} \cdot 1 = \frac{8}{15}\)
Step 5: Multiply by 2: \(2 \times \frac{8}{15} = \frac{16}{15}\)
\(\boxed{\displaystyle \frac{16}{15}}\)

Solution 3: \(\displaystyle \int \sin^5 x \, dx\)
Step 1: Write \(\sin^5 x = \sin^4 x \cdot \sin x = (1-\cos^2 x)^2 \sin x\).
Step 2: Substitute \(u = \cos x\), then \(du = -\sin x \, dx\).
Step 3: Integral becomes \(-\int (1-u^2)^2 du = -\int (1 - 2u^2 + u^4) du\).
Step 4: Integrate: \(-\left(u - \frac{2u^3}{3} + \frac{u^5}{5}\right) + C\).
Step 5: Substitute back \(u = \cos x\): \(-\cos x + \frac{2}{3}\cos^3 x - \frac{1}{5}\cos^5 x + C\).
\(\boxed{\displaystyle -\cos x + \frac{2}{3}\cos^3 x - \frac{1}{5}\cos^5 x + C}\)
↑ Back to Top
Solution 4: \(\displaystyle \int \cos^4 x \, dx\)
Step 1: Use identity \(\cos^2 x = \frac{1+\cos 2x}{2}\).
Step 2: \(\cos^4 x = \left(\frac{1+\cos 2x}{2}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{4}(1 + 2\cos 2x + \cos^2 2x)\).
Step 3: \(\cos^2 2x = \frac{1+\cos 4x}{2}\).
Step 4: \(\cos^4 x = \frac{1}{4}\left(1 + 2\cos 2x + \frac{1+\cos 4x}{2}\right) = \frac{3}{8} + \frac{1}{2}\cos 2x + \frac{1}{8}\cos 4x\).
Step 5: Integrate term by term: \(\int \frac{3}{8}dx = \frac{3}{8}x\); \(\int \frac{1}{2}\cos 2x \, dx = \frac{1}{4}\sin 2x\); \(\int \frac{1}{8}\cos 4x \, dx = \frac{1}{32}\sin 4x\).
\(\boxed{\displaystyle \frac{3}{8}x + \frac{1}{4}\sin 2x + \frac{1}{32}\sin 4x + C}\)
↑ Back to Top
Solution 5: \(\displaystyle \int_0^{\pi/2}\sin^{10}x \, dx\)
Step 1: Use reduction formula for even \(n=10\).
Step 2: \(\int_0^{\pi/2}\sin^{10}x dx = \frac{9}{10} \cdot \frac{7}{8} \cdot \frac{5}{6} \cdot \frac{3}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{\pi}{2}\).
Step 3: Multiply numerators: \(9 \times 7 \times 5 \times 3 \times 1 = 945\).
Step 4: Multiply denominators: \(10 \times 8 \times 6 \times 4 \times 2 = 3840\).
Step 5: \(\frac{945}{3840} = \frac{63}{256}\).
Step 6: Multiply by \(\frac{\pi}{2}\): \(\frac{63}{256} \times \frac{\pi}{2} = \frac{63\pi}{512}\).
\(\boxed{\displaystyle \frac{63\pi}{512}}\)

Solution 6: \(\displaystyle \int x^2 e^{ax} \, dx\)
Step 1: Use integration by parts: \(\int u dv = uv - \int v du\).
Step 2: Let \(u = x^2\), \(dv = e^{ax}dx\). Then \(du = 2x dx\), \(v = \frac{e^{ax}}{a}\).
Step 3: \(\int x^2 e^{ax}dx = \frac{x^2 e^{ax}}{a} - \frac{2}{a}\int x e^{ax}dx\).
Step 4: For \(\int x e^{ax}dx\), let \(u = x\), \(dv = e^{ax}dx\). Then \(du = dx\), \(v = \frac{e^{ax}}{a}\).
Step 5: \(\int x e^{ax}dx = \frac{x e^{ax}}{a} - \frac{1}{a}\int e^{ax}dx = \frac{x e^{ax}}{a} - \frac{e^{ax}}{a^2}\).
Step 6: Substitute back: \(\frac{x^2 e^{ax}}{a} - \frac{2}{a}\left(\frac{x e^{ax}}{a} - \frac{e^{ax}}{a^2}\right) = \frac{e^{ax}}{a}\left(x^2 - \frac{2x}{a} + \frac{2}{a^2}\right) + C\).
\(\boxed{\displaystyle \frac{e^{ax}}{a}\left(x^2 - \frac{2x}{a} + \frac{2}{a^2}\right) + C}\)

Solution 7: \(\displaystyle \int_0^{\pi/4}\sin^4 2x \, dx\)

Step 2: Limits: \(x=0 \Rightarrow t=0\); \(x=\frac{\pi}{4} \Rightarrow t=\frac{\pi}{2}\).
Step 3: Integral = \(\frac{1}{2}\int_0^{\pi/2} \sin^4 t \, dt\).
Step 4: For \(n=4\): \(\int_0^{\pi/2}\sin^4 t dt = \frac{3}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{\pi}{2} = \frac{3\pi}{16}\).
Step 5: Multiply by \(\frac{1}{2}\): \(\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{3\pi}{16} = \frac{3\pi}{32}\).
\(\boxed{\displaystyle \frac{3\pi}{32}}\)

Solution 9: \(\displaystyle \int \sin^4 x \, dx\)
Step 1: Use identity \(\sin^2 x = \frac{1-\cos 2x}{2}\).
Step 2: \(\sin^4 x = \left(\frac{1-\cos 2x}{2}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{4}(1 - 2\cos 2x + \cos^2 2x)\).
Step 3: \(\cos^2 2x = \frac{1+\cos 4x}{2}\).
Step 4: \(\sin^4 x = \frac{1}{4}\left(1 - 2\cos 2x + \frac{1+\cos 4x}{2}\right) = \frac{3}{8} - \frac{1}{2}\cos 2x + \frac{1}{8}\cos 4x\).
Step 5: Integrate: \(\frac{3}{8}x - \frac{1}{4}\sin 2x + \frac{1}{32}\sin 4x + C\).
\(\boxed{\displaystyle \frac{3}{8}x - \frac{1}{4}\sin 2x + \frac{1}{32}\sin 4x + C}\)

Solution 10: Prove \(u_n + n(n-1)u_{n-2} = n\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right)^{n-1}\)
Step 1: \(u_n = \int_0^{\pi/2} x^n \sin x \, dx\). Integrate by parts: \(u = x^n\), \(dv = \sin x dx\).
Step 2: \(du = n x^{n-1}dx\), \(v = -\cos x\).
Step 3: \(u_n = \left[-x^n \cos x\right]_0^{\pi/2} + n\int_0^{\pi/2} x^{n-1} \cos x dx\).
Step 4: Boundary term = \(0\) (since \(\cos(\pi/2)=0\) and at \(x=0\) term is 0).
Step 5: \(u_n = n\int_0^{\pi/2} x^{n-1} \cos x dx\).
Step 6: Integrate by parts again: \(u = x^{n-1}\), \(dv = \cos x dx\). Then \(du = (n-1)x^{n-2}dx\), \(v = \sin x\).
Step 7: \(\int_0^{\pi/2} x^{n-1}\cos x dx = \left[x^{n-1}\sin x\right]_0^{\pi/2} - (n-1)\int_0^{\pi/2} x^{n-2}\sin x dx\).
Step 8: At \(x=\pi/2\): \((\pi/2)^{n-1} \cdot 1\); at \(x=0\): 0. So = \((\pi/2)^{n-1} - (n-1)u_{n-2}\).
Step 9: Therefore \(u_n = n\left[(\pi/2)^{n-1} - (n-1)u_{n-2}\right]\).
Step 10: Rearranging: \(u_n + n(n-1)u_{n-2} = n(\pi/2)^{n-1}\). Hence proved.

Solution 11: Reduction formula for \(\int x^m \sin(nx) dx\)
Step 1: Integrate by parts: \(u = x^m\), \(dv = \sin(nx)dx\).
Step 2: \(du = m x^{m-1}dx\), \(v = -\frac{\cos(nx)}{n}\).
Step 3: \(\int x^m \sin(nx)dx = -\frac{x^m \cos(nx)}{n} + \frac{m}{n}\int x^{m-1} \cos(nx)dx\).
Step 4: For \(\int x^{m-1} \cos(nx)dx\), integrate by parts: \(u = x^{m-1}\), \(dv = \cos(nx)dx\).
Step 5: \(du = (m-1)x^{m-2}dx\), \(v = \frac{\sin(nx)}{n}\).
Step 6: \(\int x^{m-1}\cos(nx)dx = \frac{x^{m-1}\sin(nx)}{n} - \frac{m-1}{n}\int x^{m-2}\sin(nx)dx\).
Step 7: Substitute back to get the reduction formula.
\(\boxed{\displaystyle \int x^m \sin(nx)dx = -\frac{x^m \cos(nx)}{n} + \frac{m x^{m-1}\sin(nx)}{n^2} - \frac{m(m-1)}{n^2}\int x^{m-2}\sin(nx)dx}\)
↑ Back to Top
Solution 12: Reduction formula for \(\int \cos^n x dx\)
Step 1: Write \(\int \cos^n x dx = \int \cos x \cdot \cos^{n-1} x dx\).
Step 2: Integrate by parts: \(u = \cos^{n-1}x\), \(dv = \cos x dx\).
Step 3: \(du = -(n-1)\cos^{n-2}x \sin x dx\), \(v = \sin x\).
Step 4: \(\int \cos^n x dx = \sin x \cos^{n-1}x + (n-1)\int \sin^2 x \cos^{n-2}x dx\).
Step 5: Use \(\sin^2 x = 1 - \cos^2 x\) and simplify.
\(\boxed{\displaystyle \int \cos^n x dx = \frac{\sin x \cos^{n-1}x}{n} + \frac{n-1}{n}\int \cos^{n-2}x dx}\)

Solution 14: Reduction formula for \(\int \sin^n x dx\)
Step 1: Write \(\int \sin^n x dx = \int \sin x \cdot \sin^{n-1} x dx\).
Step 2: Integrate by parts: \(u = \sin^{n-1}x\), \(dv = \sin x dx\).
Step 3: \(du = (n-1)\sin^{n-2}x \cos x dx\), \(v = -\cos x\).
Step 4: \(\int \sin^n x dx = -\cos x \sin^{n-1}x + (n-1)\int \cos^2 x \sin^{n-2}x dx\).
Step 5: Use \(\cos^2 x = 1 - \sin^2 x\) and simplify.
\(\boxed{\displaystyle \int \sin^n x dx = -\frac{\cos x \sin^{n-1}x}{n} + \frac{n-1}{n}\int \sin^{n-2}x dx}\)

Solution 15: \(\displaystyle \int_0^{\pi/2}\sin^3\theta \cos^4\theta \, d\theta\)
Step 1: Use product formula: \(\int_0^{\pi/2}\sin^p\theta \cos^q\theta d\theta = \frac{(p-1)(p-3)\cdots (q-1)(q-3)\cdots}{(p+q)(p+q-2)\cdots} \times K\) where \(K = \frac{\pi}{2}\) if both even else \(1\).
Step 2: \(p=3\) (odd), \(q=4\) (even) \(\Rightarrow K=1\).
Step 3: Numerator: \((p-1)=2\), \((q-1)(q-3)=3\times1=3\).
Step 4: Denominator: \((p+q)=7,5,3,1\).
Step 5: \(\frac{2\times3}{7\times5\times3\times1} = \frac{6}{105} = \frac{2}{35}\).
\(\boxed{\displaystyle \frac{2}{35}}\)

Solution 17: \(\displaystyle \int \tan^4 x \, dx\)
Step 1: \(\tan^4 x = \tan^2 x (\sec^2 x - 1)\).
Step 2: \(\int \tan^4 x dx = \int \tan^2 x \sec^2 x dx - \int \tan^2 x dx\).
Step 3: Let \(u = \tan x\), \(du = \sec^2 x dx\): first integral = \(\int u^2 du = \frac{u^3}{3} = \frac{\tan^3 x}{3}\).
Step 4: \(\int \tan^2 x dx = \int (\sec^2 x - 1)dx = \tan x - x\).
\(\boxed{\displaystyle \frac{\tan^3 x}{3} - \tan x + x + C}\)

Solution 18: \(\displaystyle \int_0^{\pi/2}\sin^6 x \cos^8 x \, dx\)

Step 2: Numerator for \(p\): \((p-1)(p-3)(p-5) = 5\times3\times1 = 15\).
Step 3: Numerator for \(q\): \((q-1)(q-3)(q-5)(q-7) = 7\times5\times3\times1 = 105\).
Step 4: Denominator: \((p+q)=14,12,10,8,6,4,2\). Product = \(645120\).
Step 5: \(\frac{15\times105}{645120} = \frac{1575}{645120} = \frac{5}{2048}\).
Step 6: Multiply by \(\frac{\pi}{2}\): \(\frac{5}{2048} \times \frac{\pi}{2} = \frac{5\pi}{4096}\).
\(\boxed{\displaystyle \frac{5\pi}{4096}}\)

Solution 19: \(\displaystyle \int \tan^3 x \, dx\)
Step 1: \(\tan^3 x = \tan x (\sec^2 x - 1)\).
Step 2: \(\int \tan^3 x dx = \int \tan x \sec^2 x dx - \int \tan x dx\).
Step 3: Let \(u = \tan x\), \(du = \sec^2 x dx\): first integral = \(\int u du = \frac{u^2}{2} = \frac{\tan^2 x}{2}\).
Step 4: \(\int \tan x dx = \ln|\sec x|\).
\(\boxed{\displaystyle \frac{\tan^2 x}{2} - \ln|\sec x| + C}\)
